Jason A. Johnson

Jump to: navigation, search

Jason Adam Johnson is an American professor of political science and communications, political commentator, and writer. He is the author of the book Political Consultants and Campaigns: One Day to Sell.

Life[edit]

Johnson is currently a professor of political science and communications at Hiram College in Hiram, Ohio, where he teaches American politics, comparative politics, campaign management and communications.[1] In October 2010, Johnson was named the Politics Editor for The Source magazine. [2]

Dr. Jason Johnson is the author the book Political Consultants and Campaigns: One Day to Sell, which has been featured in Campaigns and Elections magazine and on National Public Radio.

Johnson served as campaign manager on legislative races in Virginia, South Carolina and Maryland. In the field of international and comparative politics, Johnson worked on the 2001 London mayoral campaign, and as an international election monitor in Mexico and South Africa.

Johnson earned his B.A. from the University of Virginia and his Ph.D from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill.

Johnson has been quoted as an expert on politics by such newspapers as the Wall Street Journal,[3] The Hill,[4] the Cincinnati Enquirer,[5] Akron Beacon Journal,[6] and The Plain Dealer.[7] He has also appeared in the online edition of Essence magazine and Black Enterprise Magazine.[8]

Johnson is frequent television commentator locally, nationally and internationally. He is a regular commentator on Al Jazeera English[9] and has appeared on Fox News Channel's The O'Reilly Factor.[10] In Ohio, Johnson is a regular political commentator on WKYC[11] and WOIO[12] in Cleveland, WKBN-TV[13] and WYTV[14] in Youngstown.

Johnson makes regular radio appearances on WHYY-FM in Philadelphia,[15] WCPN in Cleveland,[16] the Basheer Jones Show on WERE AM 1490 in Cleveland, and CKNW in Vancouver, British Columbia. He has appeared on National Public Radio's Morning Edition.[17]

Johnson is a regular columnist for the Chicago Defender,[18] the Michigan Chronicle,[19] the New Pittsburgh Courier,[20] and the Memphis Tri-State Defender.[21] Johnson is Senior Politics Editor of Politic365.com and has also been a regular contributor to TheLoop21.com.[22]
